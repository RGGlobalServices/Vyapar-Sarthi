# Expenses

Money going out of the shop — rent, electricity, transport, tea, wages and
anything else.

## Recording an expense

Click **Record Expense** and fill in:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Category | What kind of expense — rent, electricity, transport and so on |
| Amount | How much you spent |
| Payment Mode | **Cash**, **UPI**, **Card** or **Bank** |
| Description | Optional note so you remember what it was |

## Why the payment mode matters

Pick the mode you actually paid with. Cash expenses come out of your cash
drawer; UPI, card and bank expenses do not. Getting this right keeps your
day-end cash figure honest — if you pay the electricity bill by UPI but record
it as cash, your drawer will look short by that amount.

## Why bother

Sales alone don't tell you whether you made money. Expenses are what turn
revenue into real profit, so the more honestly you record them, the more useful
[Reports](reports.md) become.

Small daily expenses are the ones most often forgotten, and they add up over a
month. It's worth entering them as they happen rather than trying to remember
at closing time.
