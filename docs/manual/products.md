# Products

Your product catalogue — every item you sell, with its prices and barcode.

## Adding a product

Click **Add Product** and fill in the details:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Name | What you and your staff will search for |
| Category | Used for grouping and category reports |
| Brand | Optional |
| Barcode / SKU | Scan it in, or type it |
| MRP | Printed price |
| Selling Price | What you actually charge — used in billing |
| Cost Price | What you paid — used to work out profit |
| Unit | Piece, kg, litre and so on |
| Opening Stock | How many you have now |
| Minimum Level | When to warn you about low stock |

**Selling price and cost price both matter.** Selling price is what the
customer pays; cost price is what you paid. The difference is your profit, and
[Reports](reports.md) can only show profit if cost prices are filled in.

### Barcodes

You can scan a barcode with your phone camera when adding a product, so you
don't have to type it. Once saved, scanning that barcode at the counter adds
the item to the bill instantly.

If a product has no barcode, staff can still find it by name.

### Sizes and variants

Apparel and Footwear shops can store **size variants** — for example S, M, L,
or shoe sizes — each with its own stock count and, if needed, its own price.
At billing, you pick the size when adding the item.

## Managing the list

The product list shows name, category, stock, minimum level, MRP, selling
price, cost and stock value, with a status of **In Stock**, **Low Stock** or
**Out of Stock**.

- **Search** for a product by name or barcode.
- **Bulk Edit** to change many products at once instead of one by one.
- Click a product to open its details and edit it.

## Adding products while billing

You don't have to stop billing to create a product. If you scan or search for
something that isn't in the catalogue, the app offers to create it there and
then.

**Manual Add** in billing is different: it adds a one-off line to that bill
only and does **not** save the item to your catalogue. Use it for something you
sell once; create a real product for anything you'll sell again.

## Bringing in an existing catalogue

If you already have your items in a spreadsheet, don't type them in again —
see [Import Data](import.md).

## Limits

All packages allow **unlimited products**.
