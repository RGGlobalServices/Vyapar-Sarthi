# Stock

How many of each item you have, and what's running out.

## The stock screen

At the top you see counts for **Total Items**, **Low Stock** and **Out of
Stock**. The table below lists each product with its current stock, minimum
level, unit and status:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **In Stock** | Above the minimum level |
| **Low Stock** | At or below the minimum level — time to reorder |
| **Out of Stock** | Nothing left |

## Stock In and Stock Out

- **Stock In** — new goods arrived. Pick the product and enter how many.
- **Stock Out** — stock leaving for a reason other than a sale: damage,
  expiry, personal use, or correcting a miscount.

You do **not** need to record stock out for sales. Billing reduces stock
automatically every time you confirm a sale.

## Minimum level

The minimum level is what turns on the Low Stock warning. Set it to roughly
what you sell while waiting for a new delivery — if you sell 5 a day and
restocking takes 2 days, around 10 is sensible.

You can also get a **low-stock alert** notification at a time you choose — see
[Settings & Profile](settings-and-profile.md).

## Recent activity

The stock screen keeps a history of recent stock movements, so you can see what
changed and when. You can clear this history if it gets long.

## Archiving

Stopped selling something? **Archive** it rather than deleting it. Archived
products disappear from billing and the main list, but their past bills and
reports stay intact. You can **unarchive** at any time from the
**Archived Products** view.

**Delete permanently** removes the product entirely. Prefer archiving —
deletion cannot be undone.

## Warehouses (Udyog only)

Udyog shops track stock across multiple warehouses and can move stock between
them. See [Udyog (Wholesale)](udyog-wholesale.md).
